### Key Pillars of Automated Equity Research:

1. **Data Ingest Layer:** Pulling structured financial statements (Income Statement, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow) and real-time pricing data via reliable APIs (e.g., Yahoo Finance, Alpha Vantage, SEC EDGAR).

2. **Quantitative Analysis Engine:** Normalizing historical data to compute standard financial indicators (valuation, profitability, liquidity, and solvency ratios).

3. **Qualitative Rule Aggregator:** Translating mathematical indicators into contextualized textual insights (e.g., assessing whether a Debt-to-Equity ratio of 1.5 is acceptable for a capital-intensive utility company vs. a software company).

## 2. Fundamental Valuation & Metrics
To build a robust research skill, your program must compute and interpret four primary pillars of fundamental analysis:

### A. Profitability Metrics

Profitability demonstrates how efficiently a corporation converts capital into net earnings.
* **Gross Profit Margin:** $\text{Gross Profit} / \text{Revenue}$

  * *Significance:* Highlights manufacturing or direct service pricing power.

* **Return on Equity (ROE):** $\text{Net Income} / \text{Shareholder's Equity}$
  * *Significance:* Measures the efficiency of generating profits from shareholders' investments.

### B. Valuation Ratios

Valuation ratios tell your skill whether a stock is priced reasonably relative to its fundamental performance.

* **Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:** $\text{Current Share Price} / \text{Earnings Per Share (EPS)}$
  * *Significance:* Reflects how many dollars investors are willing to pay for every dollar of current company earnings.

* **Price-to-Sales (P/S) Ratio:** $\text{Market Capitalization} / \text{Total Revenue}$
  * *Significance:* Crucial for early-stage or high-growth firms with negative net income.

### C. Financial Health & Solvency

Solvency metrics protect your capital by filtering out companies under severe debt stress.
* **Current Ratio:** $\text{Current Assets} / \text{Current Liabilities}$

  * *Significance:* Assesses short-term liquidity and ability to cover obligations due within one year.

* **Debt-to-Equity (D/E) Ratio:** $\text{Total Liabilities} / \text{Total Shareholder's Equity}$
  * *Significance:* Gauges long-term capital structure and financial leverage.
